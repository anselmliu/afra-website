Global Theatre
Season 1: Global Black Women Experience
This season aims to raise awareness to the wide spectrum of ongoing generational trauma that plagues black women across the globe. This season will be comprised of the following 6 plays:
Rachel
by Angelina Weld Grimke
image source: https://www.quintessencetheatre.org/rachel
Rachel tells the story of a young black woman named Rachel who has her dreams stripped from her after she discovers the horrors of racism and how it has plagued black people for generations. After seeing and hearing cruel events unfold, Rachel falls into a deep depression, and eventually, has to make a decision that impacts the course of her life. Rachel’s story is not only one of the past, but also one of many people in today’s society, where the cycle of discrimination ceases to end.
Blue Blood
by Georgia Douglas Johnson
Blue Blood does a phenomenal job at exploring how women, and especially black women, have been abused and taken advantage of by men in power. Even wealthy black women like Mrs. Temple find themselves in traumatic situations that they can’t escape. This play may resonate with any group today who has had their voices silenced.
Color Struck
by Zora Neale Hurston
image source: https://www.undermain.org/seasons-1011-current/undermain-reads-color-struck-by-zora-neale-hurston
Color Struck talks about an issue that is all too prevalent today: internalized racism. Emmaline is a black woman who can’t find herself attractive compared to lighter skinned people. This eny drives Emmaline to a breaking point and eventually, she loses much of her life to this endless battle with herself. Emmaline’s experiences may sound similar to other people of color who grew up in America.
Eclipsed
by Danai Gurira
image source: https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/why-eclipsed-danai-guriras-broadway-play-is-groundbreaking-180499/
Eclipsed is a play the solely explores the oppression and abuse that women experience, mainly sexual abuse. Three sex slaves live a horrific life, but women who aren’t sex slaves still face horrors that are inflicted by men. One of the slaves decides to free herself by becoming a soldier, only to discover the brutal violence of war. Eclipsed reminds the world that abuse towards women is a global phenomenon.
Hot Stuff
by Eulalie Spence
Hot Stuff tells the story of an impoverished woman attempting to climb the social ladder. Fanny, the protagonist of this play, finds herself defrauding clients and prostituting herself, all for materialistic items in a vein attempt to gain wealth. This lifestyle proves to be a vicious cycle of violence for Fanny, and these cycles can still be seen in impoverished areas today.
The Light
by Loy A. Webb
image source: https://mcctheater.org/tix/the-light/
The Light explores how women, especially black women, have been silenced when discussing about being victims of rape. The Light references many modern events like the Me Too movement and how men do not take accusations made by rape victims seriously. This silencing of women is something that occurs everyday in modern society.
